🏷 Workspace Terminology Config

Admin settings page where the customer defines what they call things: "Asset" → "ATM", "Collection" → "Cash Pickup", "Fill Level" → "Cash Level." The entire UI — dashboard, mobile app, notifications — dynamically reflects these labels. One database column rename in the settings table, not the schema.

📋 Configurable Check-in Forms

This is the biggest unlock. A simple form builder where admins define what drivers fill out at each stop. Drag-and-drop fields: number inputs, dropdowns, text, photo capture, checkboxes. Waste: "fill %" + photo. ATM: "cash count" + "receipt stock" + "error code" + photo. The mobile app automatically displays the right form for each task type. Unlocks every vertical.

🛠 Custom Fields on Assets

Let admins add their own fields to assets. A bin has "waste type" and "capacity." An ATM has "model number", "cash capacity", "network provider." Simple key-value store on the asset table with type validation (text, number, dropdown, date). Filterable and searchable in the dashboard.

🔄 Custom Task Types

Beyond the built-in collect/place/move. Admins define task types: "Inspection", "Restocking", "Maintenance", "Cash Pickup" — whatever their workflow needs. Each task type maps to its own check-in form and has its own icon/color in the app. Routes optimize across all task types the same way.

☰ Module Toggles

Not every vertical needs every feature. ATM operators don't need No-Go Zones. Vending companies don't need location scouting. Simple on/off switches per workspace: Route Optimization, Asset GPS, Fill Monitoring, No-Go Zones, Location Scouting, Photo Check-ins. Sidebar and dashboard adapt automatically.

🎯 Go-to-Market: Vertical First, Horizontal Second

Market Binly as "the waste logistics OS" — that's where the domain expertise lives. But when an ATM operator, vending company, or solar installer shows up, onboard them with custom config. ServiceTitan does exactly this: markets to HVAC/plumbing, but the platform works for any home service. Vertical focus, horizontal capability.

Analytics & Reporting

Turn operational data you already have into actionable dashboards

PHASE 1

Binly already collects massive amounts of data — every route, every collection, every driver GPS ping, every photo. Right now that data sits unused. Phase 1 turns it into clear dashboards and reports. Phase 2 layers on AI-driven insights that surface patterns and recommendations automatically.

📈 Operations Dashboard

Collections per driver/day/week/month. Tasks completed vs assigned. Skip rates and reasons. Average time per stop. Route completion percentages. Filterable by driver, date range, area, and asset type.

🚗 Route Efficiency Metrics

Planned miles vs actual miles driven. Planned time vs actual time. Out-of-order stops. Idle time. Dead miles (driving without a task). Before/after comparisons when route optimization is enabled. Cost per mile and cost per collection.

⛽ Asset Utilization

Bins/assets sorted by collection frequency, fill rate trends, and revenue generated. Identify underperforming assets (rarely full, low collection frequency) and overperforming ones (always full, high priority). Heatmaps by geography — where are your busiest assets?

💰 Financial Overview

Fuel spend over time using real-time gas prices. Cost per route, cost per collection, cost per driver. Revenue per route and per area. Overtime tracking. Fleet operating cost trends — weekly, monthly, quarterly views with export to CSV/PDF.

👥 Driver Performance

Leaderboard data as trends over time: safety scores, efficiency scores, tasks completed, photos taken, incidents reported. Compare drivers side-by-side. Identify training needs. Feed into gamification system.

📄 Scheduled Reports

Auto-email weekly/monthly PDF reports to managers: operations summary, fleet costs, driver rankings, asset utilization. Configurable — pick which metrics matter to you. One-click export for any dashboard view.

📺 Route Replay

Replay any completed shift on the map: see the driver's actual path vs the planned route, where they stopped, how long at each stop, where they deviated. Great for training new drivers, investigating complaints, proving service to customers, and spotting inefficiencies.

📍 Live ETA Sharing

"Driver is 3 stops away, ~25 min out." Managers (and optionally customers) can see real-time estimated arrival for any stop. Calculated from the driver's live location and remaining route. Reduces "where's my driver?" calls significantly.

🤖 AI-Driven Insights (Phase 2)

AI analyzes patterns across all your data and surfaces recommendations automatically. "Bin 23 is trending toward overflow by Thursday — schedule early pickup." "Driver A is 20% more efficient on Route 7 than Driver B — consider reassigning." "Area downtown has 3x more incidents than last month — review No-Go Zone candidates." Turns dashboards from passive reporting into proactive decision-making.

Why This Is Phase 1

Every data point for these dashboards already exists — tasks, GPS logs, shift records, fuel prices. No new data collection needed, no new hardware. Just presenting the information you already have in a clear, actionable way. High value, low effort. Every customer — waste, ATM, vending — needs analytics from day one.

AI-Powered Route Intelligence

From static optimization to predictive, self-improving routing

HIGH IMPACT

Today, routes are optimized once at shift start. Tomorrow, AI decides which assets to visit, when to visit them, and re-optimizes on the fly as conditions change throughout the day.

📈 Predictive Fill-Level Forecasting

AI trained on historical collection data — timestamps, fill levels, location, season, day of week — predicts when each asset will need service. No sensors needed. Industry studies show 24.8% reduction in redundant trips and 30.2% fewer overflow incidents.

🌟 High-Performer Bin Prioritization

Score bins by average fill rate, revenue generated, and customer importance. High-fill-rate bins get visited more frequently. Low performers get flagged for relocation. Priority scoring feeds directly into route optimization as weighted objectives.

🔄 Dynamic Mid-Shift Re-Optimization

When tasks are added or removed mid-shift, the system grabs the driver's current location, recalculates the best route for remaining stops, and pushes the updated route to the driver's phone in real-time. No manual intervention needed.

🤖 "Smart Shifts" — AI Auto-Generation

Every morning at 6am, AI analyzes predicted fill levels, driver availability, truck capacity, and pending move requests to auto-generate optimal shifts. Manager just reviews and approves. Eliminates 30–60 min of daily manual shift planning.

💰 Cost-Aware Routing + Real-Time Fuel Pricing

Factor in fuel cost, dump fees, driver wages, overtime risk, and toll roads when building routes. Live fuel prices from public gas price feeds update cost calculations automatically. Routes aren't just fast — they're the cheapest option. Docket's IronRouteAI does this — Binly should too, but with real-time gas pricing no one else has.

-25% redundant trips -30% fuel costs -30min daily planning Predictive, not reactive

Computer Vision & Photo AI

Turn every driver photo into structured intelligence

GAME CHANGER

Drivers already take photos at every bin check. Right now those photos sit in a database doing nothing. With Vision AI, every photo becomes a data point that feeds the intelligence layer.

📷 AI Fill-Level Estimation from Driver Photos

When a driver takes a check-in photo, AI vision analyzes it and estimates fill level (±5–10%). No extra hardware needed — the driver's phone IS the sensor. Compology charges $30/camera/month for dedicated sensors. Binly gets the same insights from photos already being taken. Cost: ~$0.01 per photo.

🔧 Bin Condition Assessment

Detect damage, graffiti, rust, missing lids from photos. Auto-flag bins needing maintenance or replacement. Before/after comparison for placement and move requests to verify service quality.

📊 Photo Analytics Dashboard

Aggregate photo-derived insights: fill level trends per bin, contamination hotspots by neighborhood, damage frequency by bin age. Visual proof for customer SLA reporting.

Why This Is Revolutionary

Compology and SmartEnds sell dedicated $200+ cameras installed inside dumpsters at $30/month each. Binly would achieve similar results from photos drivers are already taking — analyzed by a vision AI model at $0.01/photo. For 72 bins checked daily, that's $0.72/day vs $2,160/month for Compology. 3,000x cheaper.

Correlate asset movement with active shift schedules. Asset moves + no driver shift active = suspicious. Auto-escalate to manager with location trail map. Cross-reference with No-Go Zone incident history.

🛣 Routes

✓ Already Built

Route creation with asset assignment (collections, placements, move requests, service tasks). Map-based asset selection. Route templates. Active route monitoring with task completion tracking. Route detail view with stop sequence and driver assignment.

🛠 Planned — Phase 1

Drag-and-drop stops between routes — two-panel view (Route A ↔ Route B), select stops and move them. Map updates in real-time showing the impact. Exactly like Hauler Hero's dispatch board but with live optimization feedback.

Reorder stops within a route — drag to rearrange sequence manually. After reorder, show cost impact: "Your custom order adds 12 min and 4.2 miles vs optimized order." Let managers override the algorithm when they know better.

Per-stop time windows — set "must arrive between 9-11am" (strict) or "prefer morning" (soft) on any stop. The optimizer respects these constraints when building the route. Business hours stored per asset auto-feed as default time windows.

Workload balancing view — side-by-side: "Driver A: 28 stops, est. 6.2 hrs. Driver B: 14 stops, est. 3.1 hrs." Visual imbalance bar. One-click "Auto-Balance" redistributes stops evenly while respecting geography.

Recurring route templates — "Route 7 runs Mon/Wed/Fri at 8am, assigned to Driver A." Auto-creates shifts on schedule. Manager reviews and approves, or fully automatic if trusted. Service frequency per asset: "This asset needs 3x/week" → auto-distributed across the week.

🛠 Planned — Phase 2

"What-If" mode — experiment with stop assignments and see cost/time impact BEFORE saving. "Moving 5 stops to Driver B adds 18 min to his route but saves Driver A 25 min. Net: -7 min, -$4.20 fuel." Play with scenarios risk-free.

Optimization objective picker — choose what matters most per route: "Fastest" (min drive time), "Cheapest" (factor fuel + tolls + overtime), "Balanced" (even workload), or "Priority-First" (high-priority stops early). Different routes can use different objectives.

AI-recommended assignments — when creating a new shift, AI suggests: "Based on location clusters, driver home locations, and past efficiency, here's the optimal split across 3 drivers." Accept, tweak, or reject.

Route replay — replay any completed route on the map: actual path vs planned route, where the driver stopped, how long at each stop, deviations. Great for training and investigating complaints.

Bulk map selection — hold shift and lasso-select stops on the map, assign to a route in one click. Faster than picking from lists for geographic assignments.

🛠 Planned — Phase 3

Route comparison — compare today's routes vs last week for the same area. Fewer miles? Fewer skips? Better completion rate?

Unserviced asset alerts — "These 8 assets haven't been on any route in 7 days." Auto-suggests a route to cover them. Prevents forgotten assets.

Holiday/exception handling — mark days off on a calendar. Scheduled routes skip or redistribute to adjacent days.

Post-optimization summary — after optimization runs, show total miles, estimated fuel cost, stops per driver, and any stops that couldn't fit (capacity exceeded, time violations). Explain WHY the optimizer made certain choices.
